<section id="overview">
  <h2>Overview</h2>

  <p>
    The ACEN–UFS–merSETA programme aimed to build circular economy capabilities within the
    South African manufacturing ecosystem. The project delivered 15 integrated outputs across
    desktop research, webinar design, training framework development, African and South African
    case studies, and a comprehensive close-out report with recommendations for future CE skills
    interventions.
  </p>

  <p>
    As Project Manager, I structured the work into six coherent workstreams:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>Desktop research on CE actors, programmes, and value chains.</li>
    <li>Webinar design and delivery to introduce CE concepts and opportunities.</li>
    <li>Training frameworks and materials for engineers, manufacturers, and SMME mentors.</li>
    <li>African and South African CE case studies with critical success factors.</li>
    <li>Four live training sessions reaching engineers and SMME mentors.</li>
    <li>Final close-out reporting, lessons learned, and future recommendations.</li>
  </ul>

  <p>
    This required balancing academic expectations, sector authority requirements, and practical
    constraints in a policy-sensitive, data-limited environment.
  </p>
</section>

<section id="risk-governance">
  <h2>Risk Governance</h2>

  <h3>Hybrid PMI / Agile Risk Management</h3>

  <p>
    I designed a risk management system that combines PMI-style qualitative analysis with Agile
    delivery practices. Each risk is linked to specific deliverables and workstreams and scored
    using a 5×5 Likelihood × Impact matrix.
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>Deliverable-based risk register with 15 core risks mapped to C3–C16.</li>
    <li>Qualitative scoring (Low / Medium / High) + numerical severity bands.</li>
    <li>5×5 heatmap for stakeholder-friendly visualisation of exposure.</li>
    <li>Risk stories integrated into the Jira backlog for sprint-level mitigation.</li>
    <li>Early-warning indicators defined for each high-impact risk.</li>
  </ul>

  <p>
    Risks such as data gaps, policy shifts, low training attendance, and restricted case study
    access were prioritised and tracked iteratively, with mitigation work packaged into sprints
    and reviewed with key stakeholders.
  </p>
</section>
